Know the Signs

Signs You Need Water Heater Service

The RGV's hard water is tough on water heaters. If you notice any of these signs, do not wait for a cold shower — call us for an inspection.

No Hot Water or Not Enough

If you run out of hot water fast or get none at all, a failed element, thermostat, or pilot may be the cause — or the unit may be at the end of its life.

Rusty or Discolored Water

Brown or rusty hot water often means the tank is corroding from the inside — a sign that replacement is coming soon.

Popping or Rumbling Noises

Hard-water sediment hardens at the bottom of the tank and makes popping or rumbling sounds. It lowers efficiency and shortens the unit's life.

Water Around the Base

Pooling or moisture around the base usually means a tank leak. Tank leaks do not fix themselves — call before it becomes water damage.

10+ Years Old

Most tank heaters last 8 to 12 years, and RGV hard water shortens that. If yours is over a decade old and acting up, replacing beats repairing.

Common Questions

Water Heater FAQ

Can you replace my water heater the same day?

In most cases, yes. If your water heater fails, call us early and we will do everything we can to replace it the same day — including hauling away the old unit. Call (956) 686-3687 to check same-day availability.

Should I get a tankless or a traditional tank water heater?

It depends on your home, hot water demand, and budget. Tankless units save space and energy and provide endless hot water, but cost more upfront. Traditional tanks cost less to install and suit most homes. We will walk you through the trade-offs honestly and recommend what actually fits your household — not whatever has the biggest margin.

How long does a water heater last in the Rio Grande Valley?

Most tank water heaters last 8 to 12 years, but the RGV's hard water can shorten that by accelerating sediment buildup and corrosion. Tankless units can last 20+ years with maintenance. If yours is over 10 years old and acting up, it is usually smarter to replace than repair.

Do you install both gas and electric water heaters?

Yes. As a licensed master plumber, we install and repair gas, electric, and tankless water heaters. Gas line work is one of our core strengths, so we handle the full job safely and to code.

What are the signs I need a new water heater?

Watch for no hot water or not enough, rusty or discolored hot water, popping or rumbling noises from the tank, water pooling around the base, or an age of 10+ years. Any of these is worth an inspection. Call Valley Modern Plumbing at (956) 686-3687.
